Most Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear the most—saving you time and hassle.
Didn't find the answer you were looking for? Feel free to Contact Us.

Do you offer Seasonal Contracts?

Yes! Our seasonal contracts allow you to schedule bi-weekly or weekly visits for lawn care, snow removal, or year-round maintenance. This ensures your property stays well-maintained without the need for last-minute bookings.

How can I subscribe to a Seasonal Contract?

Reach out to us via email or phone to request a season contract. We will send you an email with a form to fillout.

How does your pricing work?

Pricing depends on the size of your property, services needed, and frequency of visits. We offer free estimates to provide a customized quote based on your specific requirements.

Are you licensed and insured?

Absolutely. We are fully licensed and insured for all services, including landscaping, snow removal, and pressure washing. Your property’s safety and our team’s professionalism are our top priorities.

How do I schedule a one-time service?

Simply call, email, or fill out our online contact form, and we’ll arrange a visit at your convenience. Same-day or emergency services may be available for urgent needs (e.g., storm cleanup).

Do you provide commercial property maintenance?

Yes! We specialize in commercial snow removal, landscaping, and pressure washing for businesses, HOAs, and rental properties. Ask about our bulk service discounts.

Can I customize my maintenance plan?

Of course! Our subscription plans are flexible—you can choose weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly visits and adjust services (e.g., lawn mowing + shrub trimming) as needed.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept credit/debit cards, checks, and bank transfers. Seasonal contracts can be paid monthly or upfront for a discount.

What if it snows unexpectedly—are you available?

Our snow removal contracts include priority service, meaning we’ll clear your property as soon as possible after snowfall. Non-contract clients are served on a first-come, first-served basis.
